  captopril 100mg Oral Tablet (captopril/Systemic) vs acetylsalicylic acid 100mg Enteric Coated Tablet (aspirin/Systemic) [Use combination with caution.]

Interaction Effect

captopril has its effect reduced by aspirin

Severity Level

Moderate - These medications may interact resulting in the potential deterioration of the patient's condition. The patient should be monitored for the possible manifestations of the interaction. Medical intervention or a change in therapy may be required.

Documentation Level

Good - Although controlled studies may not have been performed, several case reports have been documented and other data strongly suggests this interaction exists.

Probable Mechanism

Salicylates may reduce the beneficial effects of angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors (ACEIs). Salicylates inhibit synthesis of prostaglandins and may antagonise antihypertensive effects of ACEIs in a dose-dependent manner, cause vasoconstriction/reduced cardiac output in heart failure patients, as well as increased risk of renal impairment. However, studies have shown that low-dose aspirin (less than or equals to 100 mg per day) is less likely to interact with ACEI. Furthermore, there are conflicting results of aspirin’s effect to worsen heart failure in patients on concomitant ACEI. Nevertheless, if concurrent use is necessary, monitor patient’s clinical condition, blood pressure and renal function.

Actions to be taken

  1. Use combination with caution.
  2. Monitor patient clinically.
  3. Monitor renal function.
  4. Monitor blood pressure.

  captopril 100mg Oral Tablet (captopril/Systemic) vs trimethoprim 300mg Oral Tablet (trimethoprim/Systemic) [Use combination with extreme caution.]

Interaction Effect

captopril causes additive toxicity with trimethoprim

Severity Level

Moderate - These medications may interact resulting in the potential deterioration of the patient's condition. The patient should be monitored for the possible manifestations of the interaction. Medical intervention or a change in therapy may be required.

Documentation Level

Good - Although controlled studies may not have been performed, several case reports have been documented and other data strongly suggests this interaction exists.

Probable Mechanism

The concurrent use of ACE inhibitors and trimethoprim may lead to additive risk of hyperkalaemia by inhibiting the renal excretion of potassium. Meta-analyses in geriatric population found that coadministration of co-trimoxazole and an ACE inhibitor has been associated with higher risk of hospitalisation due to hyperkalaemia or sudden death. Moreover, various reports of severe hyperkalaemia and arrhythmias have been described when trimethoprim or co-trimoxazole was given concomitantly with an ACE inhibitor. Other predisposing factors include renal impairment, elderly, high dosages and diabetes. Closely monitor at-risk patient’s serum potassium levels and ECG during combined use. Consider adjusting dose or use an alternative antibiotic in elderly patients.

Actions to be taken

  1. Use combination with extreme caution.
  2. Monitor serum potassium.

  acetylsalicylic acid 100mg Enteric Coated Tablet (aspirin) vs J45 (Asthma)

Severity Level

Extreme Caution - Use of the drug is not recommended and the patient has a higher risk of developing adverse effects or exacerbation of their existing health condition, however, use is still possible and the risk may be outweighed by other considerations.

Documentation Level

Limited - Few reports of this interaction exist. These few reports usually consist of limited case reports where clinically sound justification of the interaction is found.

Probable Mechanism

Salicylates may precipitate or exacerbate asthma, and risk increases in asthmatic patients with chronic rhinitis or a history of nasal polyps. Contraindicated in severe asthma.

  acetylsalicylic acid 100mg Enteric Coated Tablet (aspirin) vs N17 (Acute renal failure)

Severity Level

Contraindicated - Absolute contraindication; the use of the drug is completely inadvisable and should be avoided.

Documentation Level

Good - Although controlled studies may not have been performed, several case reports have been documented and other data strongly suggests this interaction exists.

Probable Mechanism

Salicylates are associated with renal toxicity (e.g. nephropathy) and aspirin has been shown to prolong bleeding time in some patients with uraemia. Contraindicated in severe renal impairment.

  trimethoprim 300mg Oral Tablet (trimethoprim) vs N17 (Acute renal failure)

Severity Level

Extreme Caution - Use of the drug is not recommended and the patient has a higher risk of developing adverse effects or exacerbation of their existing health condition, however, use is still possible and the risk may be outweighed by other considerations.

Documentation Level

Good - Although controlled studies may not have been performed, several case reports have been documented and other data strongly suggests this interaction exists.
